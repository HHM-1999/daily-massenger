Glorious 70Years of Rajshahi University

Rajshahi University, the second largest university in Bangladesh located in the Rajshahi division, was established on 6 July 1953. Popularly known as Cambridge of the East, the university is in a 753-acre campus in Motihar, 5 kilometers from the Rajshahi city centre. In terms of academic activities it is the second largest and second oldest university in Bangladesh. Researchers of this university recently put significant effort and played a key role in bringing back Bangladeshi Muslin fiber.

The Election Commission of FBCCI election has disqualified 29 top businessmen due to debt and tax default in the organization’s election. Illegal sand mining threatens roads, croplands in Bandarban 

In Bandarban`s Petainyachara area, located under Lama Upazila, an alarming environmental crisis unfolds as illegal sand extraction operates relentlessly from dawn to dusk. This widespread activity poses a significant threat to croplands, bridges, culverts, roads, as well as numerous public and private structures. At three distinct locations within the area, four sand mining machines relentlessly excavate and extract sand from the `Kumari Chara` region without any regard for regulations. The unlawfully acquired sand is then clandestinely transported via 10 to 15 dumper vehicles to multiple destinations in Lama-Chakaria upazila.
